# CardStats product vision

## Vision statement

**CardStats helps every person discover, compare, and understand the real eligibility, cost, and evidence behind any crypto card before trusting it with money.**

## Mission

Maintain the most auditable global record of crypto payment cards and turn fragmented, fast-changing terms into transparent, scenario-based decisions.

## The job to be done

When someone wants to spend digital assets through a card, help them determine:

1. which products are genuinely available to them;
2. how funds move and who holds or lends them;
3. what the product will cost under their behavior;
4. which rewards are realistically attainable;
5. what can go wrong and who is responsible;
6. how current and authoritative each answer is.

The product is successful when users no longer need ten issuer tabs and a spreadsheet—and still feel more informed, not less.

## Strategic position

This is not a card blog, an affiliate coupon site, an onchain dashboard, or a wallet. It is a **decision and evidence platform** with three connected products:

- a free consumer comparison experience;
- an internal research and verification operating system;
- eventually, a licensed data and change-intelligence service.

The consumer interface creates distribution and feedback. The research system creates quality. The data service creates defensible recurring revenue. Building only the first creates a replaceable SEO property.

## Product promises

### We will say what we know

Every critical field has a value state: known, not offered, not disclosed, not applicable, conflicting, or stale. “Free” is never inferred from a blank value.

### We will say why we know it

Users can inspect the official source, source type, relevant location, observation date, and last human review for important claims.

### We will not hide the denominator

Analytics state coverage: programs, regions, dates, tracking methods, and missing data. Catalog claims distinguish live, waitlisted, paused, and sunset products.

### We will not sell “best”

Results are conditioned on user inputs and show the reasons, tradeoffs, and excluded alternatives. Commercial relationships are separate from ranking.

### We will preserve changes

Material terms are not silently overwritten. Users can see what changed, when it was observed, when it became effective, and whether the issuer gave advance notice.

## Differentiation

| Common category behavior | Our position |
|---|---|
| One row per card brand | Program, regional/legal offering, tier, and time are separate |
| Maximum reward headline | Attainable reward range after caps, exclusions, token haircut, and conditions |
| “Available globally” | Residency/citizenship rule, lifecycle, evidence, and confidence per jurisdiction |
| Page-level “last updated” | Field-level observation and verification |
| One opaque rating | Separate data confidence, economics, resilience evidence, and user signal |
| Overwrite old terms | Public temporal record and change alerts |
| Affiliate-first ordering | Neutral ranking function and audited commercial separation |
| Scrape and publish | Automated discovery, typed validation, and accountable human review |

## Competitive moat

The interface is not the moat. Filters and comparison tables can be copied. The defensible assets are:

1. **Temporal evidence graph:** normalized claims tied to official artifacts and two notions of time—when a term applied and when it was observed.
2. **Research operations:** source registry, change monitors, exception handling, review SLAs, and corrections.
3. **Entity resolution:** knowledge of brands, legal issuers, processors, program managers, networks, regions, and product variants.
4. **Decision models:** continuously tested effective-cost and eligibility logic with explicit assumptions.
5. **Historical corpus:** program changes, pauses, closures, reward dilution, and issuer transitions that cannot be recreated instantly.
6. **Contributor relationships:** issuer contacts, subject-matter reviewers, and users who submit verifiable corrections.
7. **Distribution loops:** alerts, saved decisions, cited research, API integrations, and recurring category reports.

A large scraped table without these qualities is a liability, not a moat.

## Product principles and hard choices

- **Accuracy beats theoretical completeness.** Publish 50 strong records before 200 weak ones.
- **Official terms beat marketing.** Cardholder agreements and fee schedules outrank landing-page copy.
- **Scope beats ambiguity.** A claim without country, tier, and effective date is not normalized.
- **Human judgment remains accountable.** Models may extract and classify; they do not publish critical changes unattended.
- **Privacy is a feature.** Country and scenario discovery can work without an account; precise financial profiles stay local or are explicitly saved.
- **Trust is not purchasable.** Issuers may correct facts for free. No paid verification badge.
- **Neutrality is measurable.** Partner and nonpartner products receive the same data requirements and ranking logic.
- **Explain risk; do not pronounce safety.** Regulatory status, incidents, recourse, and data confidence are facts or bounded assessments—not a guarantee.

## North-star and guardrails

### North-star metric

**Qualified decision completions (QDC):** sessions in which an eligible user compares at least two offerings or completes a finder result, inspects at least one evidence or cost explanation, and then saves, shares, confirms at issuer, or deliberately rejects all options.

This is better than outbound clicks because it measures informed decisions, including “none is suitable.”

### Quality guardrails

- Critical-claim freshness coverage.
- Critical fields with authoritative evidence.
- Confirmed correction rate per 1,000 claims.
- Median change detection and publication time.
- Conflicting claims unresolved beyond SLA.
- Partner/nonpartner rank and coverage parity.
- User comprehension of cost, eligibility, and custody in testing.
- Complaint rate about misrepresented availability or fees.

### Business metrics

Repeat visitors, alert retention, organic/direct mix, issuer correction participation, qualified outbound rate, affiliate revenue per QDC, data-customer retention, and research cost per verified offering.

## Long-term roadmap

### Horizon 1: trusted catalog

Prove that country-first discovery, comparisons, and evidence are materially better than issuer-tab research. Build the research console and a 40–60 program gold set.

### Horizon 2: decision engine

Add scenario value, change alerts, broader jurisdictions, use-case research, saved shortlists, and carefully controlled review evidence.

### Horizon 3: market intelligence

Publish historical benchmarks, program resilience indicators, licensed or independently indexed adoption analytics, and a B2B API.

### Horizon 4: category infrastructure

Offer a versioned crypto-card schema, issuer submission protocol, independent data certification, decision widgets, and machine-readable change feeds. The company can become the reference layer used by wallets, travel products, researchers, and regulators.

## Boundaries

The company should not initially:

- issue a card, custody assets, route applications, or recommend leverage;
- promise tax, legal, or individualized financial advice;
- rank products by paid placement;
- sell user financial scenarios to issuers;
- scrape personal data or republish licensed analytics without rights;
- call a program safe based on a single composite score;
- support every country before local research and disclosures are credible.

These constraints protect focus and independence. Issuing or brokerage economics may look attractive later, but they would fundamentally change regulation and trust.

## Twelve-month definition of success

At month twelve, success is not “the largest database.” It is:

- 100+ programs with published inclusion and freshness coverage;
- the top target-country results reviewed within policy;
- measurable user comprehension and repeat use;
- a public correction and change ledger that issuers engage with;
- diversified acquisition, with direct/return traffic and alerts reducing search dependence;
- initial affiliate revenue without observed ranking bias;
- at least three design partners validating willingness to pay for the data/change API.

If users do not inspect evidence or change behavior after seeing total costs, the differentiation thesis is not working and the company should not merely add more cards.
