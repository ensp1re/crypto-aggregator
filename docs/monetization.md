# Monetization strategy

## Thesis

The product can support affiliate, sponsorship, premium, and data revenue, but monetization must arrive in an order that protects the evidence brand. The most valuable long-term revenue is likely B2B data/change intelligence; affiliate revenue can finance it earlier.

No revenue stream may purchase inclusion, verification, a score, a correction outcome, or organic rank.

## Revenue sequence

### 1. Affiliate referrals — after neutral utility is proven

Some issuers operate referral or affiliate programs; availability and geography change. For example, Bybit publishes [card referral](https://www.bybit.com/en/help-center/article/FAQ-Bybit-Card-Referral-Program) and [general affiliate](https://www.bybit.com/en/help-center/article/Bybit-Affiliate-Program-General-FAQ) information. These examples demonstrate feasibility, not guaranteed inventory or permission for every market.

Operating rules:

- Include eligible nonpartners with equal data depth and ranking logic.
- Always provide a direct official confirmation link.
- Label partner links next to the call to action, not only in a footer.
- Use `rel="sponsored"` and region-appropriate disclosures.
- Store partner metadata outside product facts and rank inputs.
- Publish affiliate-neutrality methodology and monitor rank/coverage parity.
- Do not show an application CTA where availability/critical facts are stale or conflicting.
- Record gross approval/reversal economics separately from editorial analytics.

The US [FTC endorsement guidance](https://consumer.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/ftcs-endorsement-guides-what-people-are-asking) calls for clear and conspicuous disclosure. Other markets have their own standards.

### 2. Sponsorship — clearly separated inventory

Possible inventory: newsletter sponsorship, report underwriting, event/webinar sponsorship, or visually distinct brand messages. Sponsors cannot sponsor a “best” label, evidence badge, comparison position, or allegedly independent review.

Reports should disclose funding and retain methodology/editorial control. An issuer can fund broad research only if unfavorable findings remain publishable.

### 3. B2B data and change feeds — primary moat monetization

Customers may include wallets, exchanges, travel/fintech apps, research firms, compliance teams, issuers, program managers, and media. Products:

- current offerings and eligibility API;
- fee/reward/limit normalized feed;
- source provenance and confidence metadata;
- lifecycle and material-change webhook;
- provider relationship/concentration data;
- historical snapshots and benchmark reports;
- embeddable neutral comparison modules.

[Paymentscan's data API](https://paymentscan.xyz/data-api), advertised at roughly $999–$1,999 per month during research, is a useful market signal for crypto-card analytics. It does not prove willingness to pay for our distinct dataset.

Begin with three to five design partners and manual exports. Do not build enterprise entitlements before validating which fields and SLAs matter.

### 4. Consumer premium — retention, not paywalled truth

Potential paid features: multi-program change alerts, saved scenario history, advanced exports, portfolio/backup-card planning, tax-event categorization aids, and high-spend tier optimization. Core eligibility, fees, evidence, warnings, corrections, and methodology remain free.

Expected consumer willingness may be low and episodic. Test annual rather than monthly plans and do not make this the base-case business.

### 5. Advertising — last resort

Generic display advertising degrades performance and trust and is unlikely to be attractive at niche scale. If used, prohibit deceptive crypto offers, token promotions, leverage products, and ad formats that resemble rankings or issuer facts.

## Illustrative affiliate economics

These are scenario models, not forecasts. Real approval, payout, region, reversal, and consent rates must be validated.

| Scenario | Monthly visits | High-intent share | Partner click rate | Approved/paid rate | Net payout | Monthly revenue |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Conservative | 50,000 | 15% | 8% | 3% | $30 | $540 |
| Base | 100,000 | 20% | 12% | 5% | $40 | $4,800 |
| Strong | 500,000 | 25% | 15% | 6% | $60 | $67,500 |

Formula: visits × high-intent share × partner click rate × paid conversion × net payout.

Sensitivity is extreme. A program closure, geo mismatch, clawback, search update, or payout change can erase most revenue. Model cash collection lag and reversals, not just approved applications.

## Illustrative B2B path

- Design partner: free/low-cost custom feed in exchange for structured requirements and referenceability.
- Starter API: $300–$750/month for limited calls/current data, after data quality is proven.
- Professional: $1,000–$3,000/month for history, changes, provenance, and support.
- Enterprise: custom licensing, redistribution, SLA, and data scope.

At five customers paying $500–$2,000, B2B contributes $2,500–$10,000 MRR and diversifies affiliate dependence. Pricing must reflect redistribution rights, update SLA, support, and liability—not rows delivered.

## Business model benchmark

[NerdWallet's 2025 annual filing](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1625278/000162527826000014/nrds-20251231.htm) reports $836.6 million revenue, including $416.9 million performance marketing, demonstrating that consumer research can monetize at scale. It also highlights dependence on marketing partners and search distribution. Those economics should be read as category proof, not a comparable forecast.

## Commercial governance

### Structural separation

- Head of Research owns inclusion, source hierarchy, factual publication, and methodology.
- Growth owns partner relationships and link placement within fixed rules.
- Ranking code cannot read affiliate tables.
- Quarterly audit compares partner and nonpartner coverage, freshness, rank, CTA visibility, and correction times.
- Material commercial conflicts appear on issuer/program pages.
- Staff compensation for research does not depend on issuer-level conversions.

### Disclosures

Provide a plain-language global disclosure and jurisdiction-specific additions. Identify when the company may receive money, how that does not affect ranking, whether the market is incomplete, and that users must confirm issuer terms.

For UK consumers, cryptoasset financial promotions can have broad application, including overseas communications. Review the [FCA crypto marketing regime](https://www.fca.org.uk/firms/cryptoassets/marketing-uk-consumers) and its [guidance](https://www.fca.org.uk/publications/fg23-3-finalised-non-handbook-guidance-cryptoasset-financial-promotions) with qualified counsel before targeting or linking offers. “Comparison” does not automatically exempt promotional content.

## Issuer services that are acceptable

- Free correction and structured data submission.
- Paid API access to the same published data.
- Paid implementation support for feeds/widgets.
- Clearly labeled sponsored research with editorial independence.
- Aggregate, privacy-safe market insights.

## Issuer services to reject

- Paid verification badge.
- Removal of unfavorable verified facts or reviews.
- Guaranteed rank, “recommended,” or “best” status.
- Faster factual correction only for paying customers.
- Exclusive data restrictions that make consumer coverage incomplete.
- Sale of identifiable user scenarios or correction submitter information.

## Risks

- Affiliate programs are geographically restricted, volatile, and can close without notice.
- High-risk crypto advertisers can create legal and reputational exposure.
- Partner incentives can subtly change copy, selection, or default filters.
- Search dependence creates revenue concentration.
- B2B customers require accuracy, uptime, rights, and liability commitments before the organization is ready.
- Sponsorship can contaminate research even with disclosure.
- Premium users may churn after a one-time card decision.

## Defensibility

Affiliate links are not defensible. Defensibility comes from costly-to-recreate history, source relationships, normalized regional terms, correction operations, scenario logic, integration contracts, and trust. Revenue should finance those assets.

## Validation plan and gates

1. Before affiliates: prove QDC and partner-neutral ranking tests with at least 40 verified programs.
2. Interview ten issuers/program managers and ten potential API users; obtain three written design-partner commitments before API build.
3. Test disclosed official versus partner link placement for comprehension, not click maximization alone.
4. Model contribution margin after research labor, payouts, reversals, legal, and support.
5. Do not sign redistribution SLAs until source rights, versioning, incident response, and error policy are contract-ready.
6. Cap any issuer at 25% of revenue and organic search at 60% of qualified acquisition as long-term guardrails, then tighten as the business matures.
