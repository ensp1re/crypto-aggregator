# CardStats competitor analysis

Research date: 17 July 2026. Competitor content changes frequently; counts below are observations, not guarantees.

## Executive assessment

No researched competitor combines broad normalized coverage, exact regional eligibility, field-level provenance, scenario economics, and a public temporal record. Crypto-native sites are closest on catalog breadth or onchain analytics. Mainstream comparison sites are better at decision design, editorial governance, and monetization. Travel-money guides are better at teaching effective cost.

The winning product should combine those disciplines without copying their visual language, page templates, prose, taxonomies, rankings, or datasets.

## CryptoAgg

[CryptoAgg](https://www.cryptoagg.io/) is the direct reference competitor. The indexed catalog showed approximately 42 cards with a familiar card tile, network, name, headline cashback, annual fee, KYC label, benefit snippets, and compare action. Navigation includes browsing, discovery, comparison, and a finder.

**Strengths**

- The card metaphor is immediately legible.
- Quick scanning reduces the apparent complexity of the category.
- Finder and comparison primitives match real user intent.
- Coverage includes recognizable exchange, wallet, lending, and self-custody brands.

**Weaknesses**

- A whole program is flattened into one record even where region, network, product type, fee, or tier differs.
- Maximum cashback dominates context about caps, token requirements, exclusions, reward duration, and valuation.
- “Annual fee free” misses issuance, monthly, top-up, conversion, spread, ATM, and opportunity costs.
- “KYC required” is a blunt label that conflates identity checks, custody, funding, and privacy.
- Visible tiles do not establish country eligibility, lifecycle state, source evidence, field freshness, or confidence.
- Repeated generic card artwork creates polish but no informational value.
- Catalog size and data quality lag newer directories.

**Missing features**

Field-level source links, regional variants, effective-cost calculations, issuer/legal-entity relationships, historical terms, operational incidents, explicit unknown/conflict states, correction workflow, methodology detail, and analytical distributions.

**Opportunity**

Treat CryptoAgg as a discovery checklist, not a dataset. Its simplicity validates demand for quick scanning; the opportunity is to preserve that speed while making every important claim inspectable. Do not inherit its “one row per brand” model.

## Paymentscan

[Paymentscan](https://paymentscan.xyz/) is an analytics product, not primarily a consumer directory. Its overview presents volume, transactions, active addresses, time-series controls, rankings, issuers, chains, currencies, and payment-network share. Its [methodology](https://paymentscan.xyz/methodology) distinguishes top-ups from settlement activity, describes onchain indexing and issuer-supplied offchain data, and acknowledges address and coverage limitations. Its [data API](https://paymentscan.xyz/data-api) is a meaningful B2B benchmark, with published plans around $999 and $1,999 per month at research time.

**Strengths**

- Unique observable adoption data across selected programs and chains.
- Better methodological disclosure than most crypto-card directories.
- Time-series, rankings, chain, currency, issuer, and network views make the category tangible.
- A paid API demonstrates a possible data-business path.

**Weaknesses**

- Coverage is structurally biased toward observable onchain programs and cooperative issuers.
- An address is not a unique person; multi-address and multi-chain behavior complicates user counts.
- Top-ups can include activity unrelated to completed card purchases; some program pages disclose material non-card activity.
- Offchain and self-reported data can have different comparability and assurance from indexed data.
- The retail comparison surface is narrow and lacks exact residency, tier economics, evidence, and total cost.
- Aggregate totals can combine partial periods and heterogeneous methods.

**Missing features**

Confidence intervals or data-quality bands, comparable coverage ratios, precise retail eligibility, scenario calculators, field evidence, user recourse, program resilience, and complete catalog history.

**Opportunity**

Link to or license Paymentscan where analytics add value; do not scrape or republish its paid dataset. Label tracking method and coverage on every metric. Combine adoption context with independently verified consumer terms, and avoid naming adoption “popularity” or quality.

## CardPilled

[CardPilled](https://cardpilled.com/) claims a catalog above 120 cards, supports comparison of up to five, offers filters, a quiz, country/use-case guides, and describes weekly automated checks plus manual review and deeper monthly verification.

**Strengths**

- Strong breadth, education, and search-oriented information architecture.
- Last-verified cues and a public methodology improve trust.
- A quiz lowers the entry barrier for users who do not know comparison vocabulary.
- Guides address self-custody, travel, geography, and other high-intent needs.

**Weaknesses**

- Broad claims are difficult to audit at field level.
- Automated freshness and manual review cadence do not guarantee semantic accuracy.
- Announced, speculative, or not-yet-live products can appear alongside operating cards.
- “Ad-free” and “no affiliate” positioning is valuable but not a substitute for evidence coverage.

**Missing features**

Regional offering entities, source locators per field, contradictions, effective-value assumptions, legal entity graph, and public historical diffs.

**Opportunity**

Beat breadth with verified decision coverage: disclose how many critical fields are current, not merely how many cards exist.

## Monevia

[Monevia](https://monevia.io/) presents an editorial, data-led interface and claims roughly 193 cards with monthly verification. Comparison fields include KYC, network, product type, top-up, cost per $100, monthly limit, wallet support, and operational status. A change-log concept is particularly useful.

**Strengths**

- Effective cost and operational status are closer to actual decisions than headline rewards.
- The visual posture feels like a research publication rather than a coupon marketplace.
- Change tracking and declared verification cadence create accountability.
- Broad structured tables expose more useful attributes than typical card tiles.

**Weaknesses**

- Site-wide verification claims can conflict with visible counters or incomplete cells.
- Monthly review is too slow for high-volatility availability and fee changes.
- Field-level source authority and source location remain difficult to inspect.
- “No/minimal KYC” categories carry significant legal and data-quality risk.
- Very broad inclusion can mix live products with questionable or poorly documented programs.

**Missing features**

Bitemporal history, evidence coverage, regional legal offerings, explainable confidence, incident modeling, and personalized scenario economics.

**Opportunity**

Keep the editorial restraint and cost focus; add operational rigor and source-level inspection.

## Crypto Card Compare

[Crypto Card Compare](https://cryptocardcompare.io/) has a smaller catalog, static head-to-head pages, categories such as self-custody, cashback, travel, and staking, plus methodology, glossary, and affiliate disclosure.

**Strengths**

- Head-to-head pages match high-intent search behavior.
- Category language maps to user goals rather than database fields.
- A smaller scope makes editorial explanations more coherent.
- Affiliate disclosure is more explicit than on many comparison sites.

**Weaknesses**

- Coverage is limited and pair pages create a manual maintenance burden.
- Static comparisons can imply freshness at page-build level rather than field level.
- Country eligibility and effective costs are not sufficiently deep.
- Pairwise SEO pages can become repetitive or thin as combinations grow.

**Missing features**

Dynamic comparison URLs, regional offer modeling, scenario calculations, public diffs, evidence coverage, and program health.

**Opportunity**

Generate only comparisons with genuine demand and unique analysis; keep arbitrary combinations out of the search index.

## Curat

[Curat](https://curat.money/compare-cards) combines a large browse list, compare flow, community features, tier lists, and issuer-facing listing opportunities.

**Strengths**

- Community and tier-list mechanics can create return visits.
- Multi-card comparison is prominent.
- Issuer participation may improve discovery and commercial reach.

**Weaknesses**

- Observed catalog counts were inconsistent across surfaces.
- Apparent entity contamination—non-card brands or concepts represented as cards—undermines confidence.
- High maximum reward claims are insufficiently qualified.
- Community rankings can amplify popularity and affiliate incentives rather than suitability.
- Issuer listing and user recommendation functions create conflict-of-interest risk.

**Missing features**

Strong entity resolution, evidence hierarchy, regional terms, neutral ranking governance, moderation evidence, and temporal claims.

**Opportunity**

Community contributions should submit evidence and corrections before they submit scores. Data quality is the social loop.

## Mainstream card comparison platforms

### NerdWallet

NerdWallet demonstrates the scale of an editorial/referral marketplace. Its [2025 annual filing](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1625278/000162527826000014/nrds-20251231.htm) reports $836.6 million in revenue, including $416.9 million from performance marketing, and describes referral economics. This validates the business model, not an expected outcome for a niche startup.

**Strengths:** recognized editorial brand, comprehensive educational funnels, personalization, calculators, conversion optimization, and mature advertiser operations.

**Weaknesses:** partner economics can shape marketplace coverage; interfaces become commercially dense; largely US-centric; complex card value can be compressed into simplified labels. Its filings also document search-platform dependence. A [2025 earnings release](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1625278/000162527826000012/earningsreleaseq4fy25.htm) discussed organic-search headwinds, a warning against an SEO-only strategy.

**Opportunity:** use editorial discipline and funnel design while publishing affiliate-neutrality audits and building direct retention.

### Bankrate

[Bankrate's scoring methodology](https://www.bankrate.com/credit-cards/bankrate-score/) uses a database of more than 250 cards, category-specific weighting, and spending profiles.

**Strengths:** transparent categories, standardized evaluation, total-value thinking, and repeatable review operations.

**Weaknesses:** an “average” spending profile can hide individual fit; a single score creates false precision; affiliate density can weaken perceived neutrality.

**Opportunity:** expose scenario inputs and ranges rather than publish a universal star score.

### Finder

[Finder](https://www.finder.com.au/credit-cards) offers jurisdiction-specific comparison, filters, promoted labels, authorship, fact-checking, and update metadata.

**Strengths:** localization, structured product fields, transparent promoted placements, and strong comparison conventions.

**Weaknesses:** country silos fragment global products; partner coverage can be incomplete; “updated” metadata is usually page-level rather than claim-level.

**Opportunity:** share one global data graph while rendering jurisdiction-specific experiences and disclosures.

### WalletHub and The Points Guy

[WalletHub's methodology](https://wallethub.com/edu/cc/credit-card-rating-system/125319/) maps many metrics to user needs; [The Points Guy](https://thepointsguy.com/review-methodology/) combines quantitative value with expert and firsthand assessment.

**Strengths:** persona-aware evaluation, experiential content, and strong explanation of reward value.

**Weaknesses:** weight systems can be opaque, points valuations subjective, and commercial relationships structurally important. Both are focused on conventional US credit/travel products.

**Opportunity:** separate verified facts, modeled assumptions, and editorial judgment visually and mathematically.

## Travel comparison lessons

[MoneySavingExpert](https://www.moneysavingexpert.com/credit-cards/travel-credit-cards/) teaches the decision: pay in local currency, understand FX load, model ATM behavior, and distinguish debit from credit protections. [MoneyHelper's comparison-site guidance](https://www.moneyhelper.org.uk/en/everyday-money/insurance/how-to-buy-insurance-using-comparison-sites) warns that comparison coverage is incomplete, sponsored ordering must be understood, and first/cheapest is not always best.

The opportunity is to make these warnings structural product behavior: coverage disclosures, “why this result,” excluded alternatives, scenario inputs, and provider confirmation links.

## Product opportunities, in order

1. **Eligibility engine:** residency and lifecycle-aware results with evidence and explicit exclusions.
2. **Evidence graph:** source authority, locator, field freshness, reviewer, and disagreement.
3. **True-cost model:** conversion path, spread, fees, caps, rewards, token haircut, and capital cost.
4. **Change ledger:** important changes with before/after values and notification.
5. **Entity graph:** brand, legal issuer, program manager, processor, network, custody, and complaint route.
6. **Transparent analytics:** distributions and market structure with coverage denominators, not vanity totals.
7. **Evidence community:** corrections and issuer submissions that enter the same review queue.

## What not to copy

- Competitor datasets, wording, images, ranking labels, or proprietary taxonomies.
- Maximum cashback as the primary ordering principle.
- A universal “best card” or unexplained trust score.
- Huge catalogs populated before critical fields are verified.
- Generic card mockups that consume space without communicating terms.
- Affiliate calls-to-action that outrank provider confirmation or evidence.
- Arbitrary programmatic pair pages created only for search volume.
